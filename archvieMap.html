Kharmang, Yasin, Laspur

Community Based Conservation of Silk Route Heritages

This project focuses on the preservation of endangered heritage assets that evoke the historical 'Silk Roads' exchanges between HKH, Central Asia, and Tibet. These assets are not only of great historical significance but also bear immense cultural importance for local communities representing their identity and rich heritage once thriving in the era of Silk route exchanges. The project's scope encompasses unique heritage assets in three valleys within the HKH region.

Pinjrakari, Qajar, and Khatamband are situated in Kharmang Palace (18CE) currently inhabited by local owners showcasing unique heritage assets such as ‘Qajar’ painting (18CE), khatamband ceiling, and Pinjrakari in balcony, an art introduced by Mir Sayyid Ali Hamadani, who arrived from Persia with skilled craftsmen in the 14th century.

Gholbasher House: Situated in Yasin Valley, this endangered asset is a courtyard, dating back 600 years and until recently served as the Tukhm Rezi festival venue for the local community. It belongs to the most ancient Yaseen caste called Gholbashere and is a unique spatial feature imitating the spatial patterns of Persian madaris.

In Laspur valley, revival of an ancient silk to wool weaving tradition, known as Shu, circa 15 CE, is a traditional textile woven from fine yarn crafted by local women. The focus will be both on the practices of weaving and products produced from it.

Yasin

Revitalization of Traditional Woodcraft

This project aims to revitalize traditional woodcraft techniques in the Yasin Valley. The focus is on training local artisans in sustainable practices that respect the environment while preserving cultural heritage.

Workshops will be conducted to teach the skills of wood carving, furniture making, and the creation of traditional wooden artifacts. The project also aims to create a market for these products, ensuring that artisans can earn a sustainable income.

Laspur

Preservation of Mud Structures

This project focuses on the preservation of traditional mud structures in Laspur. The aim is to document the existing structures, assess their condition, and implement preservation techniques to ensure their longevity.

Local masons will be trained in traditional building techniques, and the project will promote the use of local materials to maintain the authenticity of the structures. Community engagement is a key component, ensuring that local knowledge and practices are respected and integrated into the preservation efforts.

Kalash

Revival of Traditional Fiber Weaving

This project aims to revive traditional fiber weaving techniques among the Kalash community. The focus is on preserving the unique patterns and methods that have been passed down through generations.

Workshops will be held to teach young artisans the skills needed to create traditional garments and textiles. The project also aims to create a sustainable market for these products, ensuring that the artisans can earn a living while preserving their cultural heritage.

Gojal

Documentation of Cultural Heritage

This project aims to document the cultural heritage of the Gojal region, focusing on the unique traditions, crafts, and practices of the local communities. The documentation will include oral histories, photographs, and written accounts of cultural practices.

The project will engage local communities in the documentation process, ensuring that their voices are heard and their stories are preserved for future generations.
